OpenAI and the Rise of Large Language Models

OpenAI was founded in 2015 by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, and others.
The company is headquartered in San Francisco and focuses on artificial intelligence research and deployment.

GPT-4 is a large language model developed by OpenAI. It was trained on massive text datasets and
demonstrates strong reasoning, coding, and language abilities. GPT-4 is used in ChatGPT, which
was launched in November 2022 and reached 100 million users in two months.

Anthropic is another AI safety company, founded by Dario Amodei and Daniela Amodei, who previously
worked at OpenAI. Anthropic developed Claude, a large language model designed with a focus on
safety and helpfulness. Claude uses Constitutional AI, a technique developed at Anthropic.

Google DeepMind was formed by the merger of Google Brain and DeepMind. DeepMind was originally
founded in London and acquired by Google in 2014. The team behind DeepMind created AlphaGo,
which defeated world champion Go player Lee Sedol in 2016. Google DeepMind also developed
Gemini, a multimodal large language model that competes with GPT-4 and Claude.

Transformer architecture, introduced by Vaswani et al. in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need",
is the foundation of all modern large language models including GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini.
The attention mechanism allows models to weigh the importance of different tokens in a sequence.

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines large language models with
external knowledge retrieval. RAG was introduced by researchers at Facebook AI (now Meta AI).
It allows language models to access up-to-date information without retraining.

LangChain is an open-source framework for building LLM applications. It was created by Harrison Chase
and provides tools for chaining LLM calls, integrating with vector databases, and building agents.
ChromaDB is a popular open-source vector database used with LangChain for semantic search.

Knowledge graphs represent information as entities and relationships. Neo4j is a graph database
company that provides tools for building and querying knowledge graphs. Microsoft uses knowledge
graphs in its Bing search engine and Azure AI services.
